A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Missile Man of India refers to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, a renowned Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam rose from humble beginnings to become one of the country's most respected figures. He played a crucial role in India's civilian space program and military missile development, leading projects like the Agni and Prithvi missiles. His work at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) earned him national recognition. Kalam was known for his visionary leadership in India's nuclear tests at Pokhran in 1998, which established India as a nuclear power. His deep commitment to science, education, and youth empowerment made him a beloved figure across generations. Even after serving as President from 2002 to 2007, he continued inspiring students through lectures and writings. Nicknamed the "People’s President," Kalam authored several books, including Wings of Fire and Ignited Minds, which continue to motivate millions. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong, leaving behind a legacy of perseverance, innovation, and service to the nation.

Biography

  • Full Name: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • Early Life: Came from a humble background; his father was a boat owner and imam. Kalam supported his family by distributing newspapers.
  • Education: Studied physics at St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and aerospace engineering at Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).
  • Career: Joined DRDO and later ISRO, where he was a key figure in India's missile development (known as the "Missile Man of India") and worked on the Pokhran-II nuclear tests
  • Presidency: Served as the 11th President of India (2002–2007); known as the "People's President" for his approachable and inspiring nature.
  • Books: Authored several motivational books, including "Wings of Fire", "Ignited Minds", and "India 2020".
  • Death: Passed away on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong; remembered as a visionary leader and great scientist.
